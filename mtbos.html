The Book of Seth

The Book of Seth initially planned as "trashbin" of ideas and place for my amateurish experiments and learning.
While this is technically still true, I started treating it more like regular project, also given nowadays almost all music made by me is under this alias.
The current plan is to finish all albums I planned for TBoS and include all music that I do in the meantime out of pure will, and then move into second experimental project with less quality baggage.

The Battle of Sancre Tor

Release date: 2022.10.11

Type: Single

Single I made in 2022 as outcome of me coming back to Elder Scrolls. Playing Beyond Skyrim's Bruma expansion, I saw the name of Sancre Tor, which brought me to read about this place.
Being quite fascinated with its lore, I decided to honour it with the song about the events happening there - as the song is instrumental, I tried to use sounds to bring symbolism as tool of narration.

Night Sessions: Towards White Cold

Release date: 2021.10.24

Type: Demo

Third and, by far, last part of Night Sessions. I think it also followed principle of being recorded in one day, but this time being entirely pre-built instead of manually played.
Thanks to that, it is the only part of the series that is publicly available and which I consider actually good. It brings a lot of nice atmosphere which I always like to include in my music.

Night Sessions: Oingo

Release date: 2020.05.15

Type: Demo

Oingo established idea of Night Sessions series as demos that are made within one day - not, unlike previously, album that is recorded quasi-live. Songs for it though, were recorded through playing them manually.
Second part of those series still has this weight of absolute amateurishness of project's beginnings, offering short trance-like experience, not especially pleasant to the ears.

Will Under Love

Release date: 2020.04.30

Type: Demo

Conceptual album that tried to play with Crowley's apothegm "Love Under Will".
As someone who consider love as primary force of humankind, I reverted it and built the demo idea around it.
Similarly to two other albums from early 2020, this represents rather poor technical skills of mine - here also supported by vocal parts that I always felt awkward about.
